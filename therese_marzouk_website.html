About Therese

She doesn't tell you
it happened for a reason.

Therese Marzouk has spent fifteen years in workforce development watching people freeze. Not because they're weak — but because nobody told them what to do with the weight while they were still carrying it.

She knows what it feels like to have a project due on Friday and a hole in your chest. To stand in a parking lot at a rest stop with a brand new dog and a grief you haven't had time to process yet. To keep feeding your kids, answering your emails, and showing up — not because you're okay, but because you don't have the option to not be okay.

"I can't do this for you. But I will be here with you while you walk through it."

What she learned — the hard way, across years of personal loss and professional reinvention — is that moving through the hard thing IS the processing. You don't have to feel ready. You just have to pick one thing and start there.

She brings that philosophy into every room she speaks in. Not as theory. As someone who has actually been frozen, and actually kept moving anyway.

Signature Keynote

Fumbling
Forward

How to keep going when you don't feel ready, don't have answers, and the avocado is already on day three.

You don't have to feel ready to take the next step. You just have to take it.

This is not a keynote about toxic positivity or hustle culture or pretending the hard thing isn't hard. It's about the person sitting in a cubicle staring at their calendar with a project due and something going on at home — and not knowing which thing to tackle first because it all feels too heavy.

Therese meets that person exactly where they are. She doesn't tell them to sit with it. She stands next to them while they figure out what's next.
